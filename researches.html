Researches

Through rigorous research in SOC operations, digital forensics, secure programming, and emerging technologies such as 6G networks and quantum cryptography, I generate insights that strengthen threat detection and incident response strategies. My work is designed to be practical and actionable, producing tested, evidence-based tools that can be directly applied to improve security posture, operational efficiency, and organizational resilience. Every study, report, or case analysis I contribute is guided by precision, reproducibility, and measurable impact—helping both teams and the broader cybersecurity community stay ahead of evolving threats.

Quantum Cryptography in 6G & AI-Driven Security

- My research explores how quantum-resistant cryptographic methods can be embedded into 6G network architectures to secure next- generation ultra-low-latency communication. I focus on integrating Quantum Key Distribution (QKD) with AI-based optimization models to dynamically manage key lifecycles, reduce entropy weaknesses, and enhance end-to-end encryption scalability.

- This work delivers measurable improvements in secure channel establishment, post-quantum threat resistance, and intelligent, self-healing communication networks.

Chaotic Systems for Secure Encryption Models

- I investigate how chaos theory and nonlinear dynamic systems can be used to design highly unpredictable, lightweight encryption schemes. By leveraging chaotic maps, irregular key streams, and sensitivity to initial conditions, I develop cryptographic models that resist statistical, brute-force, and differential attacks.

- My approach bridges theoretical mathematics with practical implementation, producing encryption techniques that are efficient, scalable, and resistant to emerging attack vectors in IoT and distributed systems.

Digital Twins for Cybersecurity Simulation & Threat Prediction

- I design and analyze digital-twin models that replicate real network environments to simulate cyberattacks and defensive strategies in a safe, controlled ecosystem. These twins integrate AI-driven threat modeling, behavioral anomaly detection, and closed-loop feedback systems to improve SOC decision-making and incident readiness.

- This research enables predictive defense, allowing organizations to test vulnerabilities, validate countermeasures, and strengthen resilience before real-world attacks occur.
